# ADR-0010: Visibility, lifecycle, and location as attestations

- **Status:** Proposed
- **Date:** 2026-05-25
- **Decision-maker:** Solo maintainer
- **Supersedes:** —

> **2026-06-15 status note ([ADR-0016](0016-vcs-native-lifecycle-mapping.md) §A).** The `visibility` field's values are renamed per ADR-0016: **`committed` → `sealed`** (removes a head-on collision with "git commit" for a VCS-native adopter) and **`published` → `public`** (the field is named `visibility`, so its values are disclosure qualities; `published` was act-shaped). Throughout this ADR, read "committed" as "sealed" and the `published` *visibility state* as `public` — meanings unchanged. The **act** "publish," the `attestation/publishes/v1` sub-type, and the *cryptographic* "commitment" noun are all **unaffected**; `visibility` is the content-disclosure axis only (one of three orthogonal dimensions per ADR-0016 §A.1 — `withdrawn`/`superseded` are lifecycle status, not visibility values). ADR-0016 also evolves this ADR additively with an optional attested `vcsRef` envelope field and an `attestation/revises/v1` lineage sub-type; this ADR's body is left intact per the append-only ADR convention.

## Context

The G2 unified-primitive cohort ([ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md)) ratified one structural envelope underlying both `content/*` and `attestation/*` families, named the [Q36](../architecture/open-questions.md#q36--attestation-sub-type-collapse-regular-family-or-structured-hierarchy) sub-type table, and formalized `nodeId` as the envelope-hash identifier that attestation references target. ADR-0009 §1 stops at structural framing + sub-type taxonomy registration; per its §3 (consequences) and the strategic memo §5 sequencing item 4, the operationalization of specific `attestation/*` sub-types — withdrawals, reinstatements, publications, locations — lands in this ADR.

The work has a primary anchor in [Q20](../architecture/open-questions.md#q20--visibility-lifecycle-and-attestpublish-semantics) (visibility lifecycle and attest/publish semantics, promoted to [civic-ai-tools#71](https://github.com/npstorey/civic-ai-tools/issues/71)). The original Q20 framing — `published` vs. `committed` visibility modes with a separate `publication-record` claim type — predates the unified-primitive taxonomy. Under [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §7's ratified table, those modes and that record type collapse into specific sub-types: visibility is expressed as the presence (or absence) of an `attestation/publishes/v1`; the publication-record IS that attestation; status changes are subsequent `attestation/withdraws/v1` / `attestation/reinstates/v1` / `attestation/supersedes/v1` nodes referencing the same target. The lifecycle is a chain of separately-signed attestation nodes — no cycle limit by construction, which closes the deferred multi-cycle refactor at `civic-ai-tools-website#58` as obsolete without ever executing it.

The work also picks up two memo findings that the unified-primitive ADR identified but did not operationalize. The **retention-asymmetry property statement** (memo §3 finding #4) names what was implicit in the prior OES §10 framing: a publisher's withdrawal authority is bounded to their own pointer and their own status label. They cannot revoke content others host or others' location-attestations. The **privacy-disclosure note** (memo §3 finding #5) names that publishing to a transparency log is itself a disclosure (hash + time + signer go public), and the architecture permits private transparency logs for maximally-sensitive cases. Both items are spec-text additions, not new mechanisms.

Three adopters are blocked without operationalizing the lifecycle/location sub-types:

**datHere** (publicly named per [ADR-0004](0004-dathere-captureMethod-variant.md); the Pittsburgh / WPRDC pilot integration partner). Their pipeline is preparing to emit publication-records and location-records across hosts (the canonical-JSON package on Vercel Blob plus the cross-host commitment view on a git host); withdrawals are already in ADR-0004's consequences. Without the location-as-attestation framing, every cross-host pointer is implicit and unsigned; without the multi-cycle-free framing, the deferred `civic-ai-tools-website#58` refactor is on their critical path.

**A second prospective collaborator in early discussions** (referenced neutrally per the workspace stakeholder/relationship-content boundary). Selective-disclosure use case: internal review of evidence packages before public release, with recipient-distributed pre-publication review packages. The visibility chain (`attestation/publishes/v1` lands later; the content node exists earlier; intermediate attestations may circulate among reviewers) maps directly onto the operationalized lifecycle. Without the zero-location private/draft base case (§5 below) and the two-atomic-Rekor-entry publication property (§6 below), there is no way to express "signed, transparency-logged, but not publicly located."

**A third prospective collaborator in early discussions** (also referenced neutrally per the boundary). Federation use case: mirrors, archives, alternative hosts each emit their own `attestation/locatedAt/v1` referencing the same content node. Without location-as-attestation, mirrors are implicit and verifiers can't distinguish "the publisher's own pointer" from "an independent durable copy." Multiple `(signer.identifier, uri-authority)` pairs is the structural expression of independence (per [Q38](../architecture/open-questions.md#q38--dedicated-copyof-relation-vs-multiple-locatedat-attestations) resolved on arrival in ADR-0009 §7 refinement (c)).

The Xanadu gate is satisfied. Three named adopters are blocked on the same operationalization scope — multi-publisher lifecycle (datHere), selective-disclosure pre-publication flow (second collaborator), and cross-host location attestations (third collaborator). The cohort lands as one ADR because the four sub-types (`withdraws`, `reinstates`, `publishes`, `locatedAt`) are semantically coupled — they're the lifecycle/location subset of the [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §7 table — and the retention-asymmetry + privacy-disclosure + zero-location + two-Rekor-entry decisions span all four. Splitting would fragment the OES §10 rewrite across multiple commits.

This ADR closes [Q20](../architecture/open-questions.md#q20--visibility-lifecycle-and-attestpublish-semantics) at the spec level. Implementation tracking continues on [civic-ai-tools#71](https://github.com/npstorey/civic-ai-tools/issues/71) per its acceptance criteria (ADR + spec amendment + reference implementation); the ADR + spec amendment land here, and impl is a Phase 3 item scoped separately per the Consequences section below. This ADR does NOT close [Q22](../architecture/open-questions.md#q22--host-as-typeable-subject--host-self-attestation-shape) (host self-attestation shape) — host policy and host endorsement framings live in the `content/host/v1` / `content/hostPolicy/v1` / `attestation/endorses/v1` sub-types per ADR-0009 §7's Q36 collapse finding; that work tracks separately under proposed-issue 008 when a real host-self-attestation adopter blocks.

This ADR is also consistent with [Q15](../architecture/open-questions.md#q15--external-verification-testing) staying the top priority gate: the two-atomic-Rekor-entry property (§6 below) and the embedded-proofs direction in location-attestations make Q15 more reachable, but shipping the cohort is not a substitute for actually running the external-verification test. The test stays scoped as a follow-on milestone.

## Decision

Operationalize the lifecycle/location sub-set of the [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §7 attestation table: status changes (withdrawals, reinstatements, supersession) become separately-signed `attestation/*` nodes per the ratified payload shapes; the publisher's own URL and any backup-host URL become signed `attestation/locatedAt/v1` nodes; the BlobRef pattern in OES §4.5 becomes the single-signer implicit case of the same verification rule; a publisher's withdrawal authority is named as bounded to their own pointer and status label; publishing to a transparency log is named as itself a disclosure with private-log substrate permitted; a content node with zero `locatedAt` attestations is the valid private/draft/enterprise base case; and publication is named as the two-atomic-Rekor-entry property (content node + locatedAt attestation, each independently inclusion-proven).

### 1. Status-as-attestation (operationalizes the lifecycle sub-types)

The OES §10 DB-row framing — `withdrawnAt`, `withdrawnReason`, `withdrawalSignature`, `withdrawalTimestamp`, `reinstatedAt`, `reinstatedReason`, `reinstatementSignature`, `reinstatementTimestamp` — is replaced at the spec level by separately-signed `attestation/*` nodes referencing the original `content/*` node by `nodeId`. The lifecycle sub-types operationalized in this ADR are:

- **`attestation/withdraws/v1`** — the publisher signs an attestation referencing the target node by `nodeId`, carrying a `reason` (required, non-empty) and `effectiveAt` (defaults to envelope timestamp). Authorization rule per [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §5: `publisher-only` — the signer of the withdrawal MUST be the publisher of the target node (i.e., `signer.identifier` MUST match the target node's `signer.identifier`).
- **`attestation/reinstates/v1`** — the publisher signs an attestation referencing the target node by `nodeId` AND the prior withdrawal by `priorWithdrawalNodeId`, optionally carrying a `reason`. Authorization rule: `publisher-only`.
- **`attestation/supersedes/v1`** — the publisher signs an attestation referencing the prior node by `nodeId` (the `targetNodeId` field) and a successor node by `successorNodeId`. Authorization rule: `publisher-only` (typically the same publisher; delegated-publisher relationships per [Q20](../architecture/open-questions.md#q20--visibility-lifecycle-and-attestpublish-semantics) are a future extension).
- **`attestation/publishes/v1`** — the publisher signs an attestation transitioning the target node's visibility from "committed" (signed + transparency-logged but no public location) to "published" (signed + transparency-logged + at least one public `attestation/locatedAt/v1`). Payload fields per [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §7: `targetNodeId`, `publicationHost`, `releasedAt`. Authorization rule: `publisher-only` OR `delegated-publisher` per Q20; the delegated-publisher rule's identity-binding mechanics are scoped to a future ADR.

Payload shapes are as ratified by [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §7's table; this ADR operationalizes them rather than re-ratifying.

The full lifecycle of a node is a **chain of attestation nodes**, each referencing the previous by `nodeId` (for reinstatement, via `priorWithdrawalNodeId`; for supersession, via `successorNodeId`). Multi-cycle support — `published → withdrawn → reinstated → withdrawn → reinstated → ...` — is **free by construction**: each cycle adds two more attestation nodes to the chain, with no cycle counter, no DB-column-shape mutation, and no spec-level cycle limit. The deferred multi-cycle refactor at `civic-ai-tools-website#58` closes as obsolete; the engineering need it tracked (any sequence of lifecycle actions, with history preserved) is satisfied structurally by the attestation chain.

A verifier processing a `content/*` node MUST surface the chain in lifecycle order (envelope timestamp; ties broken by `nodeId` lexicographic) and report the current lifecycle status derived from the latest signer-matched lifecycle attestation per the retention-asymmetry rule in §3 below. The DB-row fields on the reference implementation's `evidence_records` table continue to be honored as a deprecated-but-still-supported source of withdrawal state for pre-ADR-0010 packages; one-time migration to attestation nodes happens at Phase 3 implementation time (see Consequences).

### 2. Location-as-attestation (operationalizes `attestation/locatedAt/v1` + reframes BlobRef)

The publisher's own URL where their content lives — currently encoded implicitly in the reference implementation as the Vercel Blob URL stored on the database row — becomes a **signed `attestation/locatedAt/v1`** referencing the content node by `nodeId`. Backup hosts (mirrors, archives, alternative-host deployments) emit their own `attestation/locatedAt/v1` referencing the same `nodeId`, signed by their own keys. Multiple `attestation/locatedAt/v1` attestations from different `(signer.identifier, uri-authority)` pairs express that the content has **independent durable copies** per [Q38](../architecture/open-questions.md#q38--dedicated-copyof-relation-vs-multiple-locatedat-attestations) resolved-on-arrival.

Payload fields per [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §7: `targetNodeId`, `uri`, `contentHash` (multihash; SHOULD match the target node's `contentHash` — mismatch is informative, indicating content drift between the location and the target's signed fingerprint), optional `contentLength`, optional `availability`. Authorization rule: `any-with-binding` — any signer with an identity binding of at least pseudonymous tier MAY emit a `locatedAt` for any content node; the consumer-side filter on `signer.bindingTier` and `signer.identifier` is the right place to weight signals. (Specifically: a `locatedAt` whose signer matches the target's publisher carries first-asserter weight; a `locatedAt` whose signer is a recognized archive/mirror carries durability weight; a `locatedAt` whose signer is unknown carries no inherent weight.)

The **BlobRef pattern in OES §4.5** (the inline four-field JSON object `{ ref, url, contentType, size }` substituting for inline content in `output`, `trace`, `skillMetadata.skillText`) is the **single-signer implicit case** of `attestation/locatedAt/v1`'s verification rule. The verification mechanics are structurally identical: fetch the URL over HTTPS, recompute the content hash over the fetched bytes, compare against the signed fingerprint, confirm size. The differences are placement and signing surface — BlobRef is an in-envelope field shape covered by the parent node's signature (the publisher implicitly asserts location-and-fingerprint as part of their own signed package); `attestation/locatedAt/v1` is a separately-signed envelope of its own, which means it can be added later (after the content node is signed), by other parties, or as part of multi-host federation. BlobRef-shaped substitution remains conformant under v0.1 for pre-existing sub-content references; new cross-host location declarations SHOULD use `attestation/locatedAt/v1` to gain the separately-signable, third-party-extensible surface. The OES §4.5 rewrite below frames this without breaking existing packages.

### 3. Retention-asymmetry property statement

A publisher's withdrawal authority is **bounded**: a `publisher-only` lifecycle attestation (`withdraws`, `reinstates`, `supersedes`) controls the publisher's own pointer and the publisher's own status label, NOT content others host. Concretely:

- A `attestation/withdraws/v1` from publisher P referencing target T removes P's own visibility commitment (if P had emitted an `attestation/publishes/v1` previously, that publication is logically reversed for P-as-asserter). It does NOT invalidate `attestation/locatedAt/v1` attestations signed by other parties pointing at T's content hosted on other hosts. The signature on P's withdrawal is mathematically intact regardless of where the content lives.
- A backup-host's `attestation/locatedAt/v1` remains independently verifiable after P's withdrawal: the backup host's signature still verifies; the content at their URI still hashes correctly; the backup host's location attestation has not been retracted (only P's own status has). Whether the backup host honors P's withdrawal at the application layer is a host-policy question, not a protocol question.
- A verifier MUST surface the **latest signer-matched lifecycle attestation alongside any verified copy**. Operationally: when rendering a content node with an `attestation/withdraws/v1` from P AND an `attestation/locatedAt/v1` from backup-host B, the verifier displays both — "withdrawn by publisher; copy still available at B's host" — rather than silently treating the withdrawal as global content erasure.

This is the deliberate civic-accountability feature the prior OES §10 implied but did not name. Silent erasure of civic-data claims is a worse failure mode than asymmetric retention; the standard surfaces the asymmetry honestly and lets consumers apply judgment (per OES §2's normative preamble).

A future ADR may operationalize per-host policy expressions where a backup host commits in advance to honoring publisher withdrawals (a host-policy attestation under `content/hostPolicy/v1` per Q22); the protocol does not assume that today. The retention-asymmetry property is the absence of a global takedown mechanism, not the presence of an opt-out mechanism.

### 4. Privacy-disclosure note (transparency log is itself a disclosure)

Publishing a node to a transparency log (Sigstore Rekor or any equivalent) is itself a public act: the envelope hash (`nodeId`), the envelope timestamp, and the signer's `kid` (and therefore — via the trust registry's `signerIdentity` per [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §4 — the signer's identity) all become public records the moment the inclusion proof is obtained. This is the intended property for `published` analyses where transparency is a feature; it is not a neutral act for sensitive or pre-publication content.

The architecture therefore **permits private transparency logs** for maximally-sensitive cases: an organizational-internal Rekor-equivalent log; a recipient-distributed inclusion-proof protocol where only the intended audience can verify the proof; or a deferred-publication pattern where the public log entry is created only when the publication transition lands. None of these are built; all are design-permitted under the same `attestation/*` envelope contract.

This is a half-sentence to half-paragraph addition to OES §6.2 and a design-permission statement in this ADR. It is **Xanadu-gated for implementation**: no private-log substrate ships until a real adopter is blocked. The note exists to (a) make the disclosure honest to current adopters reasoning about whether to publish, and (b) anchor a future implementation path without committing to a specific private-log mechanism now.

### 5. Zero-location private/draft/enterprise base case

A `content/*` node with **no `attestation/locatedAt/v1` referencing it** is a valid private content node. The signer holds the bytes; no public location is asserted; the content node may or may not have a public transparency-log inclusion proof (per §4 above). This is the structural base case for:

- **Draft / pre-publication** content: signed and timestamped, kept locally or in private storage until the publisher transitions to published.
- **Enterprise-private** content: the publisher's organization holds the content; no public host serves it; identity binding may be against an internal identity provider (subject to [Q3](../architecture/open-questions.md#q3--first-non-github-identity-provider)).
- **Recipient-distributed** content: the publisher distributes the bytes directly to specific recipients (encrypted to recipient keys, or transported via a side channel); no public `locatedAt` is emitted. This is the selective-disclosure pattern the second collaborator's pipeline anchors.

Zero-location is a valid state, not an error. A verifier encountering a content node with zero `locatedAt` attestations does not report `unknown_target` or any equivalent — there is nothing to verify against in the location dimension, by design. The other verifier checks (envelope integrity, signature mathematics, content hash, type resolution, signer identity cross-check, timestamp, transparency-log inclusion if present) apply normally; absent a public location, the content-hash check is satisfied against whatever bytes the verifier has obtained out-of-band (a recipient-held copy; the publisher's own local store; etc.).

The transition from zero-location to located-at-public-URL is exactly an `attestation/publishes/v1` (the visibility transition; §1 above) typically paired with at least one `attestation/locatedAt/v1` (the public pointer; §2 above). The two attestations together constitute the publication act — see §6 below.

### 6. Publication as two atomic Rekor entries

A **publication** is structurally the emission of two coupled signed nodes, each of which independently transparency-log-includes:

1. The **content node** (e.g., `content/analysis/v1`) signed by the publisher and inclusion-proven on the transparency log. This commits the content's identity (`nodeId`) and integrity (`contentHash`) to the public record. If the content node was previously a zero-location private node (§5 above), the inclusion proof is the first public artifact.
2. The **`attestation/locatedAt/v1`** signed by the publisher (the first asserter) and inclusion-proven on the transparency log. This commits the public pointer (`uri`) to the public record, alongside a fingerprint check.

Each of the two attestations is independently verifiable. A verifier can:

- Verify the content node alone (envelope integrity, signature, content hash, type, signer, timestamp, transparency-log inclusion) without ever fetching the `locatedAt` — yielding a "valid content, location unspecified" verdict.
- Verify the `locatedAt` alone (envelope integrity, signature, signer identity, timestamp, transparency-log inclusion, fetch and content-hash compare against the `targetNodeId`'s claimed `contentHash`) without fetching the original content node from the publisher — yielding a "this URI serves content matching `nodeId` X" verdict.
- Verify both together — yielding a "publisher signed both, transparency log includes both, content lives at the asserted URI" verdict.

A publisher MAY emit the two attestations in either order; the publication is logically complete when both are inclusion-proven. The publisher's own pipeline emits both as part of a single publish flow; a backup host's `locatedAt` is a third, separately-signed entry emitted later, by a different signer, against the same target. There is no upper bound on `locatedAt` attestations per content node; each one is an additional public assertion of durable availability.

This property is the structural answer to [Q15](../architecture/open-questions.md#q15--external-verification-testing): once both attestations carry their inclusion proofs embedded (which is the OES §13.3 target end-state direction), an external verifier with the content node + `locatedAt` attestation + the public trust registry + FreeTSA + Sigstore Rekor can complete every §13.1 check without `civicaitools.org`. Embedding the proofs in the attestation envelopes themselves is a Phase 3 implementation item per the strategic memo §5 sequencing; ADR-0010 specifies the structural shape that makes the property reachable.

## Considered and rejected alternatives

- **Keep OES §10's DB-row framing in parallel with the new attestation-based framing (transition gradually).** Rejected. Maintaining two state representations for the same lifecycle invites silent drift between the DB row's `withdrawnAt` column and a separately-signed `attestation/withdraws/v1` node; verifiers would have to read both and reconcile. The single-source-of-truth direction is the attestation chain; the DB-row columns are deprecated-but-honored at verification time only for pre-ADR-0010 packages, with a one-time migration emitting equivalent attestation nodes when Phase 3 implementation lands. Future packages do not write the columns.

- **Mint `attestation/copyOf/v1` as a distinct sub-type for backup-host pointers.** Rejected per [Q38](../architecture/open-questions.md#q38--dedicated-copyof-relation-vs-multiple-locatedat-attestations) resolution in [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §7 refinement (c). Multiple `attestation/locatedAt/v1` from different `(signer.identifier, uri-authority)` pairs sufficiently express the "durable independent copy" property; a dedicated `copyOf` would add no structural information. Consumers and verifiers can distinguish "publisher's own pointer" from "third-party mirror" via `signer.identifier` matching the target node's publisher.

- **Specify `delegated-publisher` mechanics for `attestation/publishes/v1` in this ADR.** Rejected as scope creep. The `publisher-only OR delegated-publisher` framing in [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §7's table acknowledges that publication delegation is a valid future case (e.g., an authorized host publishes on behalf of an upstream signer), but the identity-binding mechanics of delegation (how a publisher signals which signers may delegate; how a verifier confirms the delegation chain) is itself a structural question. ADR-0010 specifies `publisher-only` as the v0.1 rule for `publishes`; delegated-publisher lands in a future ADR when an adopter needs it.

- **Make zero-location an explicit `visibility: "committed"` field on the content envelope (matching the original Q20 framing).** Rejected. Under the unified-primitive framing, visibility is not a field on the content node — it is the structural consequence of *which* attestations reference the node. A content node with no `attestation/publishes/v1` and no `attestation/locatedAt/v1` is committed-but-not-published; emitting an `attestation/publishes/v1` and at least one `attestation/locatedAt/v1` transitions to published. Encoding visibility as a content-envelope field would (a) duplicate state that already lives in the attestation chain and (b) require the content node to be re-signed (changing its `nodeId`) every time visibility changes, which breaks the "publication is two atomic Rekor entries adding to the chain" property.

- **Embed lifecycle attestations inside the content node's `extensions` map rather than as separately-signed nodes.** Rejected. Embedding would lose the independent-verifiability property of separately-signed attestations: a verifier could not check a withdrawal's signature without fetching the entire (potentially large) content node, and the withdrawal would not be independently transparency-log-includable. The two-atomic-Rekor-entry property (§6 above) and the third-party-locatable property (multi-host backup-emitter `locatedAt`s, §2 above) both depend on separately-signed envelopes.

- **Define a global takedown mechanism alongside withdrawal (e.g., a publisher's signed retraction that obligates all backup hosts to delete the content).** Rejected. A global takedown mechanism is at odds with the retention-asymmetry property (§3 above) and with the standard's broader posture that consumers apply judgment per the §2 normative preamble. A publisher who needs cross-host removal can pursue out-of-band processes (DMCA-equivalent notices, host-policy negotiations); the protocol surfaces the withdrawal honestly and lets consumers and hosts respond per their own policies. Forcing protocol-level takedown would also create a censorship vector that the standard's civic-accountability framing explicitly avoids.

- **Specify the private-transparency-log substrate in this ADR.** Rejected per the §4 framing. Naming a specific private-log mechanism (an organizational Rekor instance? a hash-only commitment to a public log without the signer's identity? a recipient-distributed inclusion proof?) prematurely commits the architecture to one implementation path. The privacy-disclosure note (§4) names that the architecture permits private logs; the specific substrate lands when a real adopter is blocked, following the Xanadu doctrine's normal promotion path.

- **Operationalize host self-attestation (`content/host/v1`, `content/hostPolicy/v1`) in this ADR alongside `attestation/locatedAt/v1`.** Rejected as scope creep. Host self-attestation is the [Q22](../architecture/open-questions.md#q22--host-as-typeable-subject--host-self-attestation-shape) work and the proposed-issue-008 deliverable; its motivating adopter is not yet blocked. The location-as-attestation framing in this ADR is independent — backup hosts emit their own `locatedAt` attestations without yet having self-declared their host identity at the protocol level. Host self-attestation lands when proposed-issue 008 is promoted.

## Consequences

- **OES amendments (paired in the same commit as this ADR).**
  - **§4.5 (BlobRef substitution)** reframed: BlobRef remains the in-envelope four-field shape `{ ref, url, contentType, size }` for sub-content references inside a parent node; explanatory paragraph added naming it as the single-signer implicit case of `attestation/locatedAt/v1`'s verification rule (fetch, hash, compare, size-check). Cross-host location declarations made by parties other than the parent node's signer SHOULD use `attestation/locatedAt/v1` (separately-signed, third-party-extensible) instead of BlobRef. Pre-ADR-0010 BlobRef-shaped fields continue to verify unchanged.
  - **§6.2 (timestamp and transparency log)** gains a privacy-disclosure paragraph: publishing to a transparency log is itself a disclosure (hash + time + signer become public). The architecture permits private transparency logs for maximally-sensitive cases; design-permitted, Xanadu-gated for implementation.
  - **§10 (withdrawal and reinstatement lifecycle)** rewritten around the `attestation/*` namespace. The lifecycle is a chain of separately-signed attestation nodes per ADR-0010 §1; the DB-row columns (`withdrawnAt`, `withdrawnReason`, `withdrawalSignature`, `withdrawalTimestamp`, `reinstatedAt`, `reinstatedReason`, `reinstatementSignature`, `reinstatementTimestamp`) become deprecated-but-honored at verification time for pre-ADR-0010 packages with a one-time migration emitting equivalent attestation nodes at Phase 3. Multi-cycle support is free by construction (no cycle counter). The retention-asymmetry property (§3 above) is stated normatively: a publisher's withdrawal controls their own pointer and status label, not content others host. New section content cross-references [ADR-0010](0010-visibility-lifecycle-location-attestations.md) and [ADR-0009](0009-unified-typed-attestation-primitive.md) §7's table.
  - **§13.1 (verification properties)** gains a check (or extends an existing one) for the lifecycle-chain rendering rule per §3 above: a verifier surfacing a content node MUST surface the chain of signer-matched lifecycle attestations referencing it, alongside any verified `locatedAt` attestations.
